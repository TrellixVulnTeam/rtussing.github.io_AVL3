Robot Design Project.

The robot design project was a semester long project for the second semester of OSU's Fundamentals of Engineering Honors class. Along with three teammates, I designed, constructed, programmed, and tested a robot (shown to the left) to move through a course and complete tasks in a fictional diner.

This was an intensive project, and it required several hundred hours as a team to complete. There is detailed documentation on the robot scenario, as well as the entire design process, available on our *Robot Project Website. As a whole, it provided a great chance to learn about managing a project and working with a team in a long-term, engineering setting.

*Currently, Ohio State does not allow the project website to be accessed publicly. If you are interested in seeing the project in full, please contact me.

M3 Challenge.

The MathWorks Math Modeling Challenge, a.k.a. the M3 Challenge, is a yearly event hosted by SIAM and sponsored by MathWorks. In this event, teams of students are given a real-world problem to solve with limited time and resources. Students compete for $100,000 in prize money, and the entire event provides an opportunity to experience the applications of mathematics. More information can be found here.

I competed in this event twice as a high school student (2020 and 2021). From my experience, it is an extremely difficult event and one that forced me to work quickly and creatively. Although we never won a prize, the competition was a great chance for my teammates and I to learn about the process of researching and solving complex problems.

Digital Flagship Swift Course.

The Digital Flagship program Ohio State University holds multiple online, self-paced courses to teach Swift, the native programming language for many Apple devices and applications. I am currently taking the Level 1 course, an introduction to Swift, XCode, and app creation. More information about these Digital Flagship programs can be found here.

In the future, I am planning on creating an application with a team of peers to practice developing applications in the Apple programming environment.
